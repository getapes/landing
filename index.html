About APES — Aerial Protection & Engagement System

APES is a pre-seed deep tech startup based in Munich, Germany, building the first hardware-agnostic, jurisdiction-adaptive counter-drone platform for civilian and commercial environments. The platform follows a Sense-Decide-Act architecture: a sensor abstraction layer that ingests data from any detection technology (RF, radar, optical, thermal, acoustic), an AI-driven classification and policy engine that evaluates threats against jurisdiction-specific rules, and an effector-agnostic response layer that orchestrates tiered countermeasures. APES is not a sensor company, not a drone manufacturer, and not a defence system — it is the operating system that sits between detection hardware and response hardware, closing the full loop autonomously. Target customers include operators of critical infrastructure, airports, energy sites, industrial facilities, and logistics hubs in the DACH region and broader Europe. The founding team combines aviation domain expertise, enterprise platform architecture (ServiceNow/ITSM), and embedded systems engineering. APES is currently pre-MVP with architecture complete, team assembled, and PoC partner pipeline active. The company is approaching seed funding to build the MVP and deploy the first proof-of-concept.

FAQ

Questions we get asked.

Whether you're evaluating counter-drone platforms, considering an investment, or exploring integration — here are the answers that matter.

APES is a counter-drone platform that closes the full Sense-Decide-Act loop for civilian and commercial environments. Unlike existing solutions — which typically provide only detection (sensors), only mitigation (effectors), or defence-grade systems inaccessible to commercial buyers — APES integrates all three layers into a single, automated operating system. It is hardware-agnostic (works with any sensor or effector), jurisdiction-adaptive (the compliance engine adapts to local regulations), and delivered as SaaS per protected site.

APES is designed for operators of critical infrastructure, industrial facilities, airports, logistics hubs, energy sites, and any commercial environment where unauthorized drones pose operational, safety, or security risks. The primary target market is the DACH B2B enterprise segment, with broader European ambitions.

Hardware-agnostic means APES does not manufacture or require specific sensors or effectors. The Sense layer ingests data from any detection technology — RF, radar, optical, thermal, acoustic — through a unified abstraction layer. The Act layer can command any compliant effector. This allows operators to use existing hardware investments or select best-of-breed components for their specific environment.

APES includes a jurisdiction-adaptive compliance engine at its core. The Decide layer evaluates detected threats against location-specific rules and response policies. In jurisdictions where active countermeasures are permitted, APES can orchestrate the full Sense-Decide-Act cycle. In jurisdictions where only detection is legally allowed, the platform delivers standalone Sense and Decide capabilities — detection, classification, and intelligence — which have independent commercial value.

APES is pre-seed and pre-MVP. The full platform architecture (Sense-Decide-Act) has been designed end-to-end, the founding team is assembled, regulatory research is complete, and the PoC partner pipeline is active. The seed round will fund MVP development and first proof-of-concept deployment — potentially in a jurisdiction that permits full-cycle engagement.

APES is based in Munich, Germany, with engineering presence across Europe and Ukraine. The founding team combines aviation domain expertise (CEO), enterprise platform architecture with 12+ years of ServiceNow and ITSM leadership (CTO), and applied sciences engineering bridging embedded systems and client delivery (Technology Advisor). This convergence of aviation, orchestration, and embedded systems expertise is uniquely suited to building a platform-layer solution for airspace security.
